GOAL 6: CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION

Goal 6 aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Water and sanitation are critical to the health of people and the planet. Goal 6 not only addresses the issues relating to drinking water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH), but also the quality and sustainability of water resources worldwide. Improvements in drinking water, sanitation and hygiene are essential for progress in other areas of development too, such as nutrition, education, health and gender equality.

CHALLENGES

Challenges we're facing

Clean water integration

Between 2015 and 2022, the population using safely managed drinking water increased by 4%, the population using safely managed sanitation increased by 8% and the population using basic hygiene services increased by 8%. Achieving our goals by 2030 will require respective increases of 6x, 5x, and 3x in the current global rates of progress.

Lacking of permanent water

While the world has experienced a net increase in permanent surface water between 2005 and 2022, losses of permanent water in some regions linked to droughts and increased water demands should be recognized.

Financial burdens

Water use efficiency rose from $17.4/m3 in 2015 to $20.8/m3 in 2021, a 19% increase. At the global level, the water stress level reached an average level of 18.6% in 2021 but it hides large regional variations as some regions in the world show critical water stress levels which may compromise their economic and social development. Globally, water stress level has slightly increased by 3% from 2015 to 2021.
